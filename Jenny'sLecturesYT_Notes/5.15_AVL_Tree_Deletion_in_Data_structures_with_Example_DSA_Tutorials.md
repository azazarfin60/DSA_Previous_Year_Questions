## 📌 Executive Summary

AVL deletion follows BST deletion, then **checks and fixes balance** using rotations. Unlike insertion (max 1 rotation), deletion may need **multiple rotations** up the tree.

---

## 📐 Deletion Steps

1. Perform **standard BST deletion**:
   - Leaf node → simply delete
   - One child → replace with child
   - Two children → replace with **inorder successor/predecessor**, then delete that node
2. Update **balance factors** bottom-up
3. If any node is unbalanced (BF > 1 or < -1), **apply rotation**

---

## 📐 Identifying Rotation After Deletion

| BF of Node | BF of Child | Rotation |
|-----------|-------------|----------|
| +2 | +1 or 0 | **LL** (R1 or R0) |
| -2 | -1 or 0 | **RR** |
| +2 | -1 | **LR** |
| -2 | +1 | **RL** |

> **BF of child = 0** case exists in deletion (not in insertion!)

---

## 📐 Example

```
Delete 60 from:
       50
      /  \
    30    60
   /  \
  20   40

Step 1: Delete 60 (leaf) → removed
Step 2: BF of 50 = 2 - 0 = +2
        BF of 30 = 1 (has both children)
        → LL rotation

After LL rotation:
       30
      /  \
    20    50
          /
         40
```

---

## ⚠️ Key Difference from Insertion

| Aspect | Insertion | Deletion |
|--------|-----------|----------|
| Max rotations | **1** | **Multiple** (up to root) |
| BF=0 on child | Not possible | Possible (R0 case) |
| May propagate | No | Yes — must check all ancestors |

---

## 💡 Key Takeaways

- Deletion: BST delete first, then rebalance
- May require **multiple rotations** (unlike insertion)
- R0 rotation (BF=0 on child) is unique to deletion
- Always check balance factors **from deleted node up to root**
