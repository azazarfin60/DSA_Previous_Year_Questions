# 5.21 Splay Trees Deletion | Bottom-up Splaying | Data Structure & Algorithm

📺 [Watch on YouTube](https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=ewRSYHStdSA)

---

## 📌 Executive Summary

This lecture covers **Splay Tree deletion** using bottom-up splaying. The process: splay the target node to root, then handle deletion based on subtree configuration.

---

## 📐 Deletion Algorithm

1. **Splay** the node to be deleted → it becomes the root
2. Remove the root → left subtree (L) and right subtree (R) remain
3. **Two cases:**
   - If L is empty → R becomes the new tree
   - If L exists → find **maximum in L** (inorder predecessor), splay it to root of L, then attach R as right child

---

## 📐 Example: Delete 15

```
Before (after splaying 15 to root):
       15
      /  \
     10   20
    / \     \
   5  12    25

Step 1: Remove root (15)
   Left subtree:    Right subtree:
       10               20
      / \                 \
     5  12                25

Step 2: Find max of left subtree = 12
        Splay 12 to root of L:
        12
       /
      10
     /
    5

Step 3: Attach R as right child of 12:
       12
      /  \
     10   20
    /       \
   5        25
```

---

## ⚠️ Alternative: Top-down Splaying
- Split tree into L and R during search
- More efficient in practice (single pass)

---

## 💡 Key Takeaways

- Splay target to root → remove → reconnect subtrees
- Find **max of left subtree**, splay it, attach right subtree
- If no left subtree → right subtree becomes tree
- Amortized O(log n) per deletion
