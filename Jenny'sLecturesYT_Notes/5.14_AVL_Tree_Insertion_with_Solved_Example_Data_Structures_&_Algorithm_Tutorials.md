## 📌 Executive Summary

AVL Tree is a **self-balancing BST** where the **balance factor** of every node is -1, 0, or +1. This lecture covers insertion with the **four rotation types** (LL, RR, LR, RL) with solved examples.

---

## 🔑 AVL Tree Definition
> An AVL tree is a BST where for every node:
> **Balance Factor (BF) = Height(Left Subtree) − Height(Right Subtree) ∈ {-1, 0, +1}**

If BF goes outside this range after insertion, **rotations** are needed to rebalance.

---

## 📐 Four Rotation Types

### 1. LL Rotation (Left-Left imbalance)
Problem: Inserted in **left subtree of left child**
Fix: **Single right rotation**

```
Before:          After:
    30 (BF=2)       20
   /               /  \
  20              10    30
 /
10

Right rotate 30 around 20
```

### 2. RR Rotation (Right-Right imbalance)
Problem: Inserted in **right subtree of right child**
Fix: **Single left rotation**

```
Before:          After:
  10 (BF=-2)       20
    \              /  \
     20          10    30
      \
       30

Left rotate 10 around 20
```

### 3. LR Rotation (Left-Right imbalance)
Problem: Inserted in **right subtree of left child**
Fix: **Left rotate child, then right rotate parent** (double rotation)

```
Before:          Step 1:          Step 2:
    30              30              25
   /               /               /  \
  20              25              20    30
   \             /
    25          20

Left rotate 20,    Right rotate 30
then right rotate 30
```

### 4. RL Rotation (Right-Left imbalance)
Problem: Inserted in **left subtree of right child**
Fix: **Right rotate child, then left rotate parent** (double rotation)

```
Before:          Step 1:          Step 2:
  10              10                15
    \               \              /  \
     20              15          10    20
    /                  \
   15                   20
```

---

## 📐 How to Identify Which Rotation

| BF of Node | BF of Child | Rotation |
|-----------|-------------|----------|
| +2 | +1 | **LL** (single right) |
| -2 | -1 | **RR** (single left) |
| +2 | -1 | **LR** (double: left then right) |
| -2 | +1 | **RL** (double: right then left) |

---

## 📐 Solved Example: Insert 21, 26, 30, 9, 4, 14, 28, 18, 15, 10, 2, 3, 7

```
Insert 21:    21

Insert 26:    21        Insert 30:    21 (BF=-2) → RR
               \                        \
                26                       26
                                          \
                                           30
After RR:     26
             /  \
            21   30

Insert 9:     26        Insert 4:     26 (check BF)
             /  \                    /  \
            21   30                21   30
           /                      /
          9                      9 (BF=+1)
                                /
                               4

BF of 21 = +2, BF of 9 = +1 → LL rotation on 21

After LL:     26
             /  \
            9    30
           / \
          4   21
```

Continue similarly for remaining elements...

## ⏱️ Complexity

| Operation | Time |
|-----------|------|
| Search | **O(log n)** |
| Insert | **O(log n)** |
| Delete | **O(log n)** |
| Space | O(n) |

---

## 💡 Key Takeaways

- AVL = BST + Balance Factor ∈ {-1, 0, +1}
- 4 rotations: LL, RR, LR, RL
- Single rotation for LL/RR, double for LR/RL
- **Guarantees O(log n)** for all operations
- After insertion, check BF bottom-up and rotate if needed
