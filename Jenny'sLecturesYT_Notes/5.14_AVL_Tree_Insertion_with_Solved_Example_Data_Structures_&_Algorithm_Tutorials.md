## 📌 Executive Summary
This lecture provides a comprehensive, step-by-step example of constructing an AVL tree from a sequence of numbers. It demonstrates all four rotation types (LL, RR, LR, RL) in a single, continuous problem, highlighting the "Median Element Trick" for quick balancing.

---

## 📐 The Problem

**Construct an AVL tree by inserting the following numbers:**
`14, 17, 11, 7, 53, 4, 13, 12, 8, 60, 19, 16, 20`

*Rule:* After every single insertion, we must check the Balance Factor (BF = Height of Left Subtree - Height of Right Subtree). If BF is not `-1, 0, or 1`, we balance immediately.

---

## 🔄 Step-by-Step Construction

### 1. Insert 14, 17, 11, 7, 53
- Insertions happen normally as per BST rules.
- Tree remains balanced.
```text
        14 (BF=1)
       /  \
 (1) 11    17 (-1)
     /      \
(0) 7        53 (0)
```

### 2. Insert 4 (Triggers LL Rotation)
- Insert `4` to the left of `7`.
- **BF Check:** Node `11` has BF = 2 - 0 = **2**. (Unbalanced!)
- **Path:** Left of 11 (`7`), Left of 7 (`4`). This is an **LL Rotation**.
- **Shortcut:** Find the median of the 3 nodes causing the imbalance `{11, 7, 4}`. Median is `7`.
- **Action:** `7` goes up, `4` goes left, `11` goes right.
```text
        14 
       /  \
      7    17 
     / \    \
    4  11    53 
```

### 3. Insert 13, then 12 (Triggers RL Rotation)
- Insert `13` to the right of `11`. (Balanced)
- Insert `12` to the left of `13`.
- **BF Check:** Node `11` has BF = 0 - 2 = **-2**. (Unbalanced!)
- **Path:** Right of 11 (`13`), Left of 13 (`12`). This is an **RL Rotation**.
- **Shortcut:** Median of `{11, 13, 12}` is `12`.
- **Action:** `12` goes up, `11` goes left, `13` goes right.
```text
        14 
       /  \
      7    17 
     / \    \
    4  12    53 
      /  \
     11  13
```

### 4. Insert 8 (Triggers RL Rotation again)
- Insert `8` to the left of `11`.
- **BF Check:** Node `7` has BF = 1 - 3 = **-2**. (Unbalanced!)
- **Path:** Right of 7 (`12`), Left of 12 (`11`). This is an **RL Rotation**.
- **Shortcut:** Median of `{7, 12, 11}` is `11`.
- **Action:** `11` goes up to replace `7`. `7` goes left, `12` goes right.
- *Reattaching orphans:* What happens to `8` (was left of 11)? Since 8 > 7, it becomes the right child of `7`.
```text
         14 
       /    \
      11     17 
     /  \      \
    7    12     53 
   / \     \
  4   8    13
```

### 5. Insert 60 (Triggers RR Rotation)
- Insert `60` to the right of `53`.
- **BF Check:** Node `17` has BF = 0 - 2 = **-2**. (Unbalanced!)
- **Path:** Right of 17 (`53`), Right of 53 (`60`). This is an **RR Rotation**.
- **Shortcut:** Median of `{17, 53, 60}` is `53`.
- **Action:** `53` goes up. `17` left, `60` right.
```text
         14 
       /    \
      11     53 
     /  \   /  \
    7   12 17   60 
   / \   \
  4   8  13
```

### 6. Insert 19, 16, then 20 (Triggers LR Rotation)
- Insert `19` (right of 17). Balanced.
- Insert `16` (left of 17). Balanced.
- Insert `20` (right of 19).
- **BF Check:** Node `53` has left height 3, right height 1. BF = 3 - 1 = **2**. (Unbalanced!)
- **Path:** Left of 53 (`17`), Right of 17 (`19`). This is an **LR Rotation**.
- **Shortcut:** Median of `{53, 17, 19}` is `19`.
- **Action:** `19` goes up to replace `53`. `17` goes left, `53` goes right.
- *Reattaching orphans:* What happens to `20` (was right of 19)? Since 20 < 53, it becomes the left child of `53`.
```text
Final Balanced AVL Tree:

          14 
       /      \
      11       19 
     /  \     /  \
    7   12   17   53 
   / \   \   /   /  \
  4   8  13 16  20  60
```

## 💡 Key Takeaway: The Median Element Trick
Instead of blindly drawing "pull down/pull up" rotations and getting confused with double rotations, use this reliable exam trick:
1. Identify the unbalanced node and the two nodes in the path of insertion causing the unbalance.
2. Arrange these **three numbers** in sorted order.
3. The **middle (median)** number becomes the new local root.
4. The smallest goes left, the largest goes right.
5. Re-attach any orphaned subtrees using standard BST rules (less than goes left, greater than goes right).
