# 5.18 Red Black Tree Deletion | DSA Tutorials

📺 [Watch on YouTube](https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=w5cvsG8kKZI)

---

## 📌 Executive Summary
Deletion in a Red-Black tree is the most complex operation. It starts with standard BST deletion. If you delete a **Red** node, no properties are violated. If you delete a **Black** node, you reduce the black-height of that path, creating a **"Double Black" (DB)** node that must be resolved through a series of 6 specific cases involving rotations and recoloring.

---

## 🔑 The Core Problem: Double Black (DB)
When a black node is deleted and replaced by its child (often a `NIL` node, which is also black), that child now holds the "blackness" of the deleted node *plus* its own blackness. 
- It becomes a **Double Black (DB)** node.
- A DB node is a violation because it counts as *two* black nodes on a path, meaning we haven't actually balanced the tree yet.
- **Goal:** Push the DB up the tree until it can be absorbed by a Red node, or until it reaches the Root.

---

## 🧩 The 6 Cases to Resolve Double Black

To resolve a DB node, look at its **Sibling (S)**.

### Case 1: DB is the Root
- **Action:** Simply remove the double blackness. It becomes a single black node. You are done!

### Case 2: Sibling (S) is BLACK, and both of S's children are BLACK
- **Analogy:** S is "cool" (black) and its children are cool. It can't help. DB asks Parent for help.
- **Action:** 
  1. Remove DB from current node.
  2. Change Sibling (S) to **RED** (it gets "angry" that DB pushed the problem up).
  3. Push the extra blackness to the Parent:
     - If Parent was **RED**, it becomes **BLACK** (Done!).
     - If Parent was **BLACK**, it becomes **DOUBLE BLACK**. Reapply the cases starting from this new DB node.

### Case 3: Sibling (S) is RED
- **Action:**
  1. Swap the colors of the Parent and Sibling (Parent becomes Red, Sibling becomes Black).
  2. Rotate the Parent in the direction of the DB node.
  3. Re-evaluate the DB node. You will now fall into Case 2, 4, 5, or 6.

### Case 4: Sibling (S) is BLACK, S's *Far* Child is BLACK, S's *Near* Child is RED
> *Near* means the child closest to the DB node. *Far* means the child furthest away.
- **Action:**
  1. Swap the colors of the Sibling and its Near Child (Sibling becomes Red, Near Child becomes Black).
  2. Rotate the Sibling in the *opposite* direction of the DB node.
  3. **Result:** You have transformed this into Case 5. Apply Case 5 immediately.

### Case 5: Sibling (S) is BLACK, S's *Far* Child is RED
- **Action:**
  1. Swap the colors of the Parent and the Sibling.
  2. Rotate the Parent in the direction of the DB node.
  3. Remove the DB status (it becomes single black).
  4. Change the *Far* Child to **BLACK**.
  5. Done!

---

## 🛠️ Step-by-Step Deletion Process

1. **Perform Standard BST Deletion:**
   - **Leaf:** Just delete it.
   - **1 Child:** Replace node with child.
   - **2 Children:** Replace node's *value* with its Inorder Successor (or Predecessor), then delete the Successor from its original spot.
2. **Check Colors:**
   - If the *deleted* node was **RED**: You are done. Tree is balanced.
   - If the *deleted* node was **BLACK** and replaced by a **RED** node: Just color the replacement node **BLACK**. Done.
   - If the *deleted* node was **BLACK** and replaced by a **BLACK** node (or `NIL`): You now have a **Double Black (DB)** node.
3. **Resolve DB:** Apply the 6 cases above until the DB is eliminated.

---

## 💡 Key Takeaway
Red-Black deletion is highly mechanical. Always start by identifying the **Sibling** of the Double Black node. The color of the Sibling and the colors of the Sibling's children completely dictate which of the strict rotation/recoloring cases you must apply.
