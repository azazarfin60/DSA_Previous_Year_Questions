# 5.11 Construct Binary Search Tree (BST) from Preorder | Example | Data Structures & Algorithm

📺 [Watch on YouTube](https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=s5RhhPPLX_w)

---

## 📌 Executive Summary
This lecture explains how to construct a unique Binary Search Tree (BST) when **only** the Preorder traversal is given. The core trick is that the Inorder traversal of a BST is always its sorted sequence.

---

## 🔑 The Core Principle
Normally, to construct a binary tree, you need two traversals (Inorder + Preorder, or Inorder + Postorder). 
If you are only given the **Preorder** for a *Binary Search Tree*:
1. **Derive Inorder:** The Inorder traversal of a BST always yields the data in **ascending (sorted) order**. So, simply sort the given Preorder array to get the Inorder traversal.
2. **Apply Standard Algorithm:** Use the standard "Construct Tree from Preorder and Inorder" method.

---

## 📐 Step-by-Step Construction

**Given Preorder:** `20, 16, 5, 18, 17, 19, 60, 85, 70`

### Step 1: Find the Inorder Traversal
Sort the Preorder sequence in ascending order:
**Inorder:** `5, 16, 17, 18, 19, 20, 60, 70, 85`

### Step 2: Build the Tree
*Rule: Preorder gives the root (first element). Inorder tells us what goes to the left and right subtrees.*

**1. Root of the whole tree:**
- First element of Preorder is **20**.
- Split Inorder at 20: 
  - Left subtree: `{5, 16, 17, 18, 19}`
  - Right subtree: `{60, 70, 85}`

**2. Left Subtree `{5, 16, 17, 18, 19}`:**
- Check Preorder: which of these comes *first*? It's **16**. So, 16 is the root of this subtree.
- Split Inorder at 16:
  - Left of 16: `{5}` → Single element, so 5 is the left child.
  - Right of 16: `{17, 18, 19}`

**3. Right of 16 `{17, 18, 19}`:**
- Check Preorder: which comes first? It's **18**. So, 18 is the root here.
- Split Inorder at 18:
  - Left of 18: `{17}` → 17 is left child.
  - Right of 18: `{19}` → 19 is right child.

*Current Left Subtree structure:*
```text
      20
     /  
   16
  /  \
 5   18
    /  \
  17    19
```

**4. Right Subtree of Root `{60, 70, 85}`:**
- Check Preorder: which comes first? It's **60**. So, 60 is the root here.
- Split Inorder at 60:
  - Left of 60: *none*
  - Right of 60: `{70, 85}`

**5. Right of 60 `{70, 85}`:**
- Check Preorder: which comes first? It's **85**. So, 85 is the root here.
- Split Inorder at 85:
  - Left of 85: `{70}` → 70 is left child.
  - Right of 85: *none*

### Final Resulting BST:
```text
          20
        /    \
      16      60
     /  \       \
    5   18      85
       /  \    /
     17   19  70
```

---

## 💡 Key Takeaways
- **Only 1 traversal is needed** if you know the tree is a BST and the given traversal is Preorder or Postorder.
- **Inorder = Sorted**.
- When choosing the root for a sub-problem, always pick the element that appears **earliest** in the given Preorder traversal.
