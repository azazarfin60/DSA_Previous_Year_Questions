## 📌 Executive Summary
B-Trees can store any comparable data, not just integers. When working with alphabetic characters, the exact same insertion and splitting algorithms apply, but comparisons are made based on **lexicographical (alphabetical) order** (A to Z).

---

## 🅰️ Alphabetic B-Tree Rules

- **Sorting:** Keys within a node must always be maintained in alphabetical order.
  - *Correct:* `[ B | D | H | K ]`
  - *Incorrect:* `[ D | H | K | B ]` (Even if B was inserted last, it must be placed in its sorted position).
- **Traversal:** Tree traversal relies on alphabetical precedence.
  - If the root is `H` and you are inserting `E`, go left (because E comes before H).
  - If you are inserting `P`, go right (because P comes after H).

## 📝 Exam Walkthrough: Alphabets in Order 5

> **Task:** Construct a B-Tree of Order 5 inserting the characters: `D, H, Z, K, B, P, Q, E, A, S, W, T, C, L, N, Y, M`.

**Parameters:** 
- Order $M = 5$. 
- Max Keys per node = $M - 1 = 4$.

### Step-by-Step Construction

**1. Insert D, H, Z, K:**
Insert into the root and sort them alphabetically.
```text
[ D | H | K | Z ]
```

**2. Insert B (Overflow):**
`B` comes before `D`. The node overflows: `[ B | D | H | K | Z ]`.
- Median element is **H**.
- Push **H** up to become the root.
```text
         [ H ]
        /     \
   [B | D]   [K | Z]
```

**3. Insert P, Q:**
Compare with `H`. Both are after `H`. Go right.
Insert into the right child. Sort them.
```text
         [ H ]
        /     \
   [B | D]   [K | P | Q | Z]
```

**4. Insert E, A:**
Compare with `H`. Both are before `H`. Go left.
Insert into the left child.
```text
            [ H ]
           /     \
   [A | B | D | E]   [K | P | Q | Z]
```

**5. Insert S (Overflow):**
Compare with `H`. Go right. Insert `S`. Right node overflows: `[K | P | Q | S | Z]`.
- Median is **Q**. Push **Q** up.
```text
         [ H | Q ]
        /    |    \
 [A|B|D|E] [K|P] [S|Z]
```

**6. Continued Insertions:**
The process continues identically. Whenever a node reaches 5 characters, find the median character (the 3rd one in the sorted list) and push it up to the parent. Ensure the children pointers are correctly maintained (characters between `H` and `Q` go to the middle child).
