# 5.27 Insertion in B-Tree of Order 4 (Left/Right Biased) | Data Structures

📺 [Watch on YouTube](https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=115K8LwP-xY) *(Note: Placeholder link)*

---

## 📌 Executive Summary
While constructing B-Trees of odd orders (e.g., 3, 5) is mathematically straightforward because there is always a single definitive median element when a node overflows, **Even Orders (e.g., 4, 6) introduce an ambiguity**.

When an even-order node overflows, there are *two* middle elements. Choosing which one to promote defines whether the B-Tree is **Left-Biased** or **Right-Biased**.

---

## ⚖️ The Even Order "Median" Problem

Let's look at a B-Tree of **Order 4 ($M = 4$)**.
- **Maximum Keys:** $M - 1 = 3$.
- Let's say we have a node `[3 | 5 | 9]`. We attempt to insert `21`.
- The node overflows: `[3 | 5 | 9 | 21]`. 

To split this node, we must push the median element up one level. However, we have **four** elements. There is no single median. The middle elements are **5** and **9**.

> [!IMPORTANT]
> **Exam Strategy: Left-Biased vs. Right-Biased**
> You must explicitly state your assumption in the exam before solving. Both are 100% mathematically correct, but you must remain consistent throughout the entire tree construction.

### 1. Left-Biased B-Tree
You consistently choose the *left* middle element to promote.
- Overflow Node: `[3 | 5 | 9 | 21]`
- Median chosen: **5**
- Root: `[5]`
- Left Child: `[3]`
- Right Child: `[9 | 21]`

### 2. Right-Biased B-Tree
You consistently choose the *right* middle element to promote.
- Overflow Node: `[3 | 5 | 9 | 21]`
- Median chosen: **9**
- Root: `[9]`
- Left Child: `[3 | 5]`
- Right Child: `[21]`

*(Note: Most university professors and textbooks default to **Left-Biased** B-Trees).*

---

## 📝 Exam Walkthrough: Order 4 (Left-Biased)

> **Task:** Construct a B-Tree of Order 4 inserting the keys: `5, 3, 21, 9, 1, 13, 2`. Assume a Left-Biased approach.

1. **Insert 5, 3:** `[3 | 5]`
2. **Insert 21:** `[3 | 5 | 21]` *(Node is full)*.
3. **Insert 9 (Overflow):** `[3 | 5 | 9 | 21]`
   - *Action:* Pick left-middle (**5**). Promote 5.
   - *Result:* Root is `[5]`. Left is `[3]`. Right is `[9 | 21]`.
4. **Insert 1:** Less than 5. Insert left.
   - *Result:* Left child becomes `[1 | 3]`.
5. **Insert 13:** Greater than 5. Insert right.
   - *Result:* Right child becomes `[9 | 13 | 21]` *(Node is full)*.
6. **Insert 2 (Overflow):** Less than 5. Insert left.
   - Left child becomes `[1 | 2 | 3 | 4]` *(Assuming 4 was inserted)*.
   - *Action:* Pick left-middle (**2**). Promote 2 to the parent node `[5]`.
   - *Result:* Root becomes `[2 | 5]`. Leftmost child is `[1]`. Middle child is `[3 | 4]`. Rightmost child is `[9 | 13 | 21]`.
