# 5.10 Binary Search Trees (BST) - Insertion and Deletion | DSA Full Course

📺 [Watch on YouTube](https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=cySVml6e_Fc)

---

## 📌 Executive Summary
This lecture covers the construction of a Binary Search Tree (BST) by inserting elements one by one, emphasizing that the *entire* left/right subtrees must obey the BST property. It also explains the three cases of node deletion: leaf node, one child, and two children (using inorder predecessor or successor).

---

## 🔑 BST Property
- A node can have at most 2 children (0, 1, or 2).
- The **left subtree** of any node contains only values **less than** that node.
- The **right subtree** of any node contains only values **greater than** that node.
> **Important:** It's not just the immediate children! Every value deep in the left subtree must be smaller than the root of that subtree.

---

## 📐 Insertion in BST

**Problem:** Construct a BST by inserting the following numbers from left to right: 
`11, 6, 8, 19, 4, 10, 5, 17, 43, 49, 31`

### Step-by-Step Trace:
1.  **11**: Becomes the **root**.
2.  **6**: `6 < 11` → insert left of 11.
3.  **8**: `8 < 11` (go left), `8 > 6` → insert right of 6.
4.  **19**: `19 > 11` → insert right of 11.
5.  **4**: `4 < 11`, `4 < 6` → insert left of 6.
6.  **10**: `10 < 11`, `10 > 6`, `10 > 8` → insert right of 8.
7.  **5**: `5 < 11`, `5 < 6`, `5 > 4` → insert right of 4.
8.  **17**: `17 > 11`, `17 < 19` → insert left of 19.
9.  **43**: `43 > 11`, `43 > 19` → insert right of 19.
10. **49**: `49 > 11`, `49 > 19`, `49 > 43` → insert right of 43.
11. **31**: `31 > 11`, `31 > 19`, `31 < 43` → insert left of 43.

### Resulting BST:
```text
          11
        /    \
       6      19
      / \    /  \
     4   8  17  43
      \   \    /  \
       5  10  31  49
```
*Note: If we were to insert `60`, it would go `>11`, `>19`, `>43`, `>49`, becoming the right child of 49.*

---

## 📐 Deletion from BST (3 Cases)

Using the tree constructed above, let's explore the three deletion cases:

### Case 1: Node has 0 Children (Leaf Node)
- **Delete 31**: Node 31 has no children. Simply remove the link from 43 and free the node. No replacement needed.

### Case 2: Node has 1 Child
- **Delete 4**: Node 4 has one right child (5). Delete node 4 and replace it with its child 5. The link from 6 will now point directly to 5.

### Case 3: Node has 2 Children
- **Delete 11 (the root)**: Node 11 has both left and right subtrees. You have two valid options for replacement:

**Option A: Replace with Inorder Predecessor**
- *Definition:* The **largest** element in the **left subtree**.
- *In our tree:* Left subtree of 11 contains {6, 4, 8, 5, 10}. The largest is **10**.
- *Action:* Replace 11 with 10.
```text
          10
        /    \
       6      19
      / \    /  \
     4   8  17  43
      \        /  \
       5      31  49
```

**Option B: Replace with Inorder Successor**
- *Definition:* The **smallest** element in the **right subtree**.
- *In our tree:* Right subtree of 11 contains {19, 17, 43, 31, 49}. The smallest is **17**.
- *Action:* Replace 11 with 17.
```text
          17
        /    \
       6      19
      / \       \
     4   8      43
      \   \    /  \
       5  10  31  49
```

---

## 💡 Key Takeaway (Trick for Predecessor/Successor)
If you struggle to find the largest in the left subtree or smallest in the right subtree visually, write down the **inorder traversal** of the tree.
> Inorder traversal of a BST always yields a **sorted sequence**.
- Sequence: `4, 5, 6, 8, 10, [11], 17, 19, 31, 43, 49`
- Element to delete: **11**
- Inorder Predecessor (just before): **10**
- Inorder Successor (just after): **17**
