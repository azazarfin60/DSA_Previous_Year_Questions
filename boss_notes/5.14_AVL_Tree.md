# 5.14 AVL Tree

> **Exam Frequency:** 🟡 High Frequency | **Typical Marks:** 04–08 | **Section:** B  
> **Course Outcome:** Maps to CO3

---

## 1. Prerequisite Knowledge
- **Binary Search Tree (BST):** A tree where left children are smaller than the parent, and right children are larger.
- **The Problem with BST:** If elements are inserted in sorted order (e.g., 10, 20, 30, 40), the tree becomes **skewed** (like a linked list). Search time degrades from O(log N) to O(N).
- **Height of a Node:** The longest path from the node to a leaf. A leaf has a height of 0 (or 1, depending on convention; class notes usually use 1 for single node).

---

## 2. Core Definitions
An **AVL Tree** is a **self-balancing Binary Search Tree** invented by Adelson-Velsky and Landis. It guarantees O(log N) time complexity for search, insert, and delete operations by maintaining strict balance.

### Balance Factor (BF)
To ensure the tree remains balanced, we calculate the Balance Factor for every node:
> **BF = Height(Left Subtree) - Height(Right Subtree)**

**The AVL Condition:** For a tree to be a valid AVL tree, the Balance Factor of *every single node* must be **-1, 0, or 1**.
- If BF is `> 1`, it means the tree is "Left Heavy".
- If BF is `< -1`, it means the tree is "Right Heavy".
- *Any node violating this rule becomes an "imbalanced node" and requires rotation.*

---

## 3. AVL Rotations
When an insertion or deletion violates the balance factor rule, we must perform a rotation on the *first unbalanced node* (moving up from the newly inserted node).

There are 4 cases of imbalance and 4 corresponding rotations:

### 3.1 LL Imbalance (Left-Left) $\rightarrow$ Right Rotation (RR)
Occurs when you insert a node into the *left* subtree of the *left* child.
- **Fix:** Perform a single **Right Rotation**.

```text
Imbalanced (Insert 10):        Right Rotation on 30:
      30 (BF=+2)                     20 (BF=0)
     /                              /  \
   20 (BF=+1)         =>          10    30
   /
 10 (BF=0)
```

### 3.2 RR Imbalance (Right-Right) $\rightarrow$ Left Rotation (LR)
Occurs when you insert a node into the *right* subtree of the *right* child.
- **Fix:** Perform a single **Left Rotation**.

```text
Imbalanced (Insert 30):        Left Rotation on 10:
   10 (BF=-2)                        20 (BF=0)
     \                              /  \
      20 (BF=-1)      =>          10    30
        \
         30 (BF=0)
```

### 3.3 LR Imbalance (Left-Right) $\rightarrow$ Left-Right Rotation
Occurs when you insert a node into the *right* subtree of the *left* child.
- **Fix:** Perform a **Left Rotation** on the left child, then a **Right Rotation** on the root.

```text
Imbalanced (Insert 20):   1. Left Rot on 10:    2. Right Rot on 30:
      30 (BF=+2)               30                    20
     /                        /                     /  \
   10 (BF=-1)      =>       20          =>        10    30
     \                     /
      20 (BF=0)          10
```

### 3.4 RL Imbalance (Right-Left) $\rightarrow$ Right-Left Rotation
Occurs when you insert a node into the *left* subtree of the *right* child.
- **Fix:** Perform a **Right Rotation** on the right child, then a **Left Rotation** on the root.

```text
Imbalanced (Insert 20):   1. Right Rot on 30:   2. Left Rot on 10:
   10 (BF=-2)                  10                    20
     \                           \                  /  \
      30 (BF=+1)   =>             20      =>      10    30
     /                              \
   20 (BF=0)                         30
```

---

## 4. Time & Space Complexity
| Operation | Time Complexity |
|---|---|
| Search | O(log N) |
| Insert | O(log N) |
| Delete | O(log N) |

*The rotations take O(1) constant time because they are just pointer changes.*

---

## 5. Exam Strategy & Practice Questions

### Typical Exam Questions:
1. **"Construct an AVL tree for the given sequence: 10, 20, 30, 25, 28, 27, 29." (08 marks)**
   - *Strategy:* THIS IS A SUREFIRE EXAM QUESTION. You must draw the tree after *every single insertion*. Whenever a node becomes unbalanced, write the BF of the nodes. Identify the type of imbalance (e.g., "RL Imbalance at node 30"). Write the rotations performed, and draw the new balanced tree before proceeding to the next insertion. 
2. **"What is the Balance Factor in an AVL tree? What are the allowed values?" (02 marks)**
   - *Strategy:* Write the formula `BF = HL - HR`. State that allowed values are `{-1, 0, 1}`. Mention that a violation triggers a rotation.
3. **"Explain the four types of rotations in an AVL tree with diagrams." (05 marks)**
   - *Strategy:* Recreate the 4 trace diagrams from Section 3. Use 3 nodes (e.g., 10, 20, 30) to keep the diagrams clean and easy to draw quickly.

### Practice Questions:
1. Insert elements 14, 17, 11, 7, 53, 4, 13 into an empty AVL tree. Show all steps and rotations.
