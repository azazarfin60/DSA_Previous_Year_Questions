# 10.9 N-Queens Problem

> **Exam Frequency:** 🟡 High Frequency | **Typical Marks:** 05–08 | **Section:** B  
> **Course Outcome:** Maps to CO4

---

## 1. Core Definition
The **N-Queens Problem** is the most famous example of a **Backtracking** algorithm.
- **The Setup:** You have an $N \times N$ chessboard.
- **The Goal:** Place $N$ chess Queens on the board such that **no two queens threaten each other**.

### The Threat Rules (Bounding Function):
A queen threatens another queen if they are in the same:
1. Row
2. Column
3. Diagonal (Primary or Secondary)

---

## 2. Working Principle (Backtracking)
Instead of placing $N$ queens randomly and checking if they attack each other (Brute Force), we use Backtracking to place them column by column.

1. Start in the leftmost column (`col = 0`).
2. If all queens are placed, return `True` (Solution found).
3. Try all rows in the current column one by one. For each row:
   a. **Check Safety:** Is it safe to place the queen at `(row, col)`? (Check left side of the row, and upper/lower left diagonals).
   b. If **Safe**, place the queen and recursively attempt to place the rest of the queens in `col + 1`.
   c. If placing the queen leads to a solution, return `True`.
   d. If placing the queen leads to a dead end (no safe spots in future columns), **BACKTRACK**. Remove the queen from `(row, col)` and try the next row.
4. If all rows have been tried and none worked, return `False`.

---

## 3. Algorithm (Pseudocode)

```
Declare solveNQUtil(int board[N][N], int col) {
    // Base case: If all queens are placed
    If col >= N Then
        Return true

    // Consider this column and try placing this queen in all rows
    For i = 0 to N - 1 do
        // Bounding Function: Check if queen can be placed on board[i][col]
        If isSafe(board, i, col) Then

            // Place this queen
            Set board[i][col] = 1

            // Recur to place rest of the queens
            If solveNQUtil(board, col + 1) Then
                Return true

            // If placing queen didn't lead to a solution, BACKTRACK
            Set board[i][col] = 0
    // If the queen can not be placed in any row in this column
    Return false

```

## 4. Trace Example (4-Queens)
Let's solve for a $4 \times 4$ board.

**Step 1 (Col 0):** Place Q1 at `(0,0)`. (Safe).
```text
Q . . .
. . . .
. . . .
. . . .
```

**Step 2 (Col 1):** Try placing Q2.
- `(0,1)`: Unsafe (Row threat)
- `(1,1)`: Unsafe (Diagonal threat)
- `(2,1)`: Safe! Place Q2 at `(2,1)`.
```text
Q . . .
. . . .
. Q . .
. . . .
```

**Step 3 (Col 2):** Try placing Q3.
- `(0,2)`: Unsafe (Row)
- `(1,2)`: Unsafe (Diagonal)
- `(2,2)`: Unsafe (Row)
- `(3,2)`: Unsafe (Diagonal)
- **DEAD END! No safe row in Col 2.**

**Step 4 (BACKTRACK):** Go back to Col 1.
- Remove Q2 from `(2,1)`.
- Try next row for Q2. `(3,1)` is Safe! Place Q2 at `(3,1)`.
```text
Q . . .
. . . .
. . . .
. Q . .
```

**Step 5 (Col 2):** Try placing Q3 again.
- `(0,2)`: Unsafe
- `(1,2)`: Safe! Place Q3 at `(1,2)`.
```text
Q . . .
. . Q .
. . . .
. Q . .
```

**Step 6 (Col 3):** Try placing Q4.
- `(0,3)`: Unsafe
- `(1,3)`: Unsafe
- `(2,3)`: Unsafe
- `(3,3)`: Unsafe
- **DEAD END! BACKTRACK.**
- ... The algorithm will eventually backtrack all the way to Q1 and move it to `(1,0)` to find the final valid solution.

## 5. Exam Strategy & Practice Questions

### Typical Exam Questions:
1. **"Explain the N-Queens problem and write the backtracking algorithm to solve it." (08 marks)**
   - *Strategy:* Define the problem rules. Write the `solveNQUtil` pseudocode. Explicitly point out where the "Backtrack" happens (`board[i][col] = 0`). You do not need to write the massive `isSafe` function unless asked; just mention it acts as the "bounding function".
2. **"Trace the placement of queens for a 4x4 board using backtracking." (05 marks)**
   - *Strategy:* Use text-based boards like the ones in Section 4. Show the first dead end explicitly to prove you understand how backtracking recovers from failures.
