# 5.13 Heap

> **Exam Frequency:** 🔴 Very High Frequency | **Typical Marks:** 05–08 | **Section:** B  
> **Course Outcome:** Maps to CO3

---

## 1. Prerequisite Knowledge
- **Complete Binary Tree:** A binary tree where all levels are fully filled except possibly the last level, which is filled from left to right.
- **Array Representation of Trees:** 
  For a node at index `i`:
  - Left Child index = `2i` (if 1-indexed)
  - Right Child index = `2i + 1` (if 1-indexed)
  - Parent index = `i / 2` (integer division)

---

## 2. Core Definition
A **Heap** is a special Tree-based data structure that satisfies two properties:
1. **Shape Property:** It must be a Complete Binary Tree.
2. **Heap Property (Order):** The value of a parent node must be consistently greater than or less than its children.

### Types of Heaps:
1. **Max Heap:** The key of the parent node is always **greater than or equal to** the keys of its children. The largest element is at the root.
2. **Min Heap:** The key of the parent node is always **less than or equal to** the keys of its children. The smallest element is at the root.

---

## 3. Insertion in a Heap (Max Heap)

### Concept
When inserting, we must maintain both the Complete Binary Tree shape and the Max Heap property.

### Algorithm (Insert)
1. Add the new element to the **end** of the array (bottom-left-most available spot in the tree) to maintain the complete binary tree shape.
2. **Heapify-Up (Bubble Up):** Compare the added element with its parent.
3. If the added element is greater than its parent, **swap** them.
4. Repeat step 3 until the element is smaller than its parent or it reaches the root.

### Trace Example: Insert 50 into Max Heap
**Current Heap:** `[40, 30, 15, 10, 20]` (Root is 40)
```text
       40
      /  \
    30    15
   /  \
  10  20
```
**Step 1: Insert 50 at the end (index 6, left child of 15)**
```text
       40
      /  \
    30    15
   / \   /
 10  20 50   <-- New
```
**Step 2: Heapify Up (Compare 50 with Parent 15. 50 > 15, Swap)**
```text
       40
      /  \
    30    50
   / \   /
 10  20 15
```
**Step 3: Heapify Up (Compare 50 with Parent 40. 50 > 40, Swap)**
```text
       50
      /  \
    30    40
   / \   /
 10  20 15
```
**Done.** 
Time Complexity: O(log N) (Height of the tree)

---

## 4. Deletion from a Heap (Delete Max)

### Concept
In a standard heap, we only ever delete the **root node** (the maximum element in a Max Heap).

### Algorithm (Delete Max)
1. Replace the root node with the **last element** in the array (the bottom-right-most node).
2. Delete the last node (reduce array size by 1).
3. **Heapify-Down (Bubble Down):** Compare the new root with its children.
4. If it is smaller than any of its children, **swap** it with the **largest child**.
5. Repeat step 4 until it is larger than both children or it becomes a leaf.

### Trace Example: Delete Max from Heap
**Current Heap:** `[50, 30, 40, 10, 20, 15]`
**Step 1: Replace Root (50) with Last Element (15). Delete 50.**
```text
       15
      /  \
    30    40
   / \
  10  20
```
**Step 2: Heapify Down (Compare 15 with children 30 and 40. Max child is 40. 15 < 40, Swap)**
```text
       40
      /  \
    30    15
   / \
  10  20
```
**Step 3: Compare 15 with its children (none). Stop.**
**Done.**
Time Complexity: O(log N)

---

## 5. Heap Sort Concept
Heap Sort works by building a Max Heap from the array, then repeatedly extracting the maximum element (Delete Max) and placing it at the end of the array.
- **Build Heap Time:** O(N)
- **Extraction Time:** O(N log N)
- **Total Time Complexity:** O(N log N)

---

## 6. Exam Strategy & Practice Questions

### Typical Exam Questions:
1. **"Construct a Max Heap from the given sequence of numbers: 10, 5, 20, 30, 15." (05 marks)**
   - *Strategy:* DO NOT just draw the final heap. Show the tree after EVERY single insertion. Draw an arrow showing the "Swap" during Heapify-Up. You get marks for showing the intermediate steps.
2. **"Explain the Delete operation in a Max Heap with an example." (04 marks)**
   - *Strategy:* State that only the root is deleted. Explain the "replace with last element" and "heapify down" algorithm. Draw a small 4-node tree to demonstrate.
3. **"What is the array representation of a heap?" (02 marks)**
   - *Strategy:* Give the formulas: Left child `2i`, Right child `2i+1`, Parent `i/2` (assuming 1-based indexing).

### Practice Questions:
1. Build a Min Heap from: `45, 20, 14, 12, 31, 7`.
2. Given the array `[50, 40, 30, 20, 10]`, prove that it is a valid Max Heap by drawing the tree.
